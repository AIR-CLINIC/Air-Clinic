About Us

BRIEF HISTORY

Air-Clinic was founded by a core team of two health-experts and a health-enthusiast who felt the need to effect considerable changes in the way the healthcare sector is handled. The Medical team is lead by Dr. George Edeh & Dr. Blessing Imade while the Project Team is lead by Mr. Ejemai. The ongoing negative trend has been the politicization of healthcare. This puts people’s lives in considerable risks because of selfish interests and ambitions at the helm of affairs. Also there are issues of high cost of healthcare, inequitable distribution, slow hospital processes, long distances to the sources of healthcare etc. These and many other reasons inspires us to decentralize the health sector.

We started off on a DISCORD COMMUNITY consisting of health-experts, health-workers, and clients. We organize daily health discussions, provide consultation services for clients, provide post-promotion channels and also provide a platform for familiarization between all these different sets of people. However, what we are doing is beyond DISCORD, that’s why we have built a world class website to carter to all the needs we must fill globally. In the nearest future we will have an APP for mobile device users to access us easily.

OUR VISION

We see a world where a major percentage of all people will be healthy because of the ease of access to healthcare. The good thing is that people will be rewarded for living healthy lives and teaching others how to live healthy too.

OFFLINE MEDICAL CONSULTATIONS

  • To decentralize healthcare
  • To provide easy, quick and affordable access to healthcare for everyone globally regardless of socioeconomic and personal barriers
  • To create a community of health experts, health enthusiasts and clients who can support each other for the sake of healthy living.
  • To increase the collaboration between health experts of different specialties and locations.
  • To provide a better, faster, more efficient method of payment for hospital transactions
  • TTo remove middlemen and bottlenecks in healthcare access.
